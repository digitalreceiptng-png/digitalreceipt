# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Nigerian individuals and businesses issuing receipts: freelancers, tutors, landlords, schools, hospitals, retailers, government contractors. A secondary audience is anyone who needs to *verify* a receipt — buyers, auditors, regulators — who arrive at the verification page without an account. Both groups need to trust the product immediately.

## Product Purpose

DigitalReceipt.ng generates tamper-proof digital receipts and makes each one independently verifiable via a unique identifier or QR code. It is Nigerian receipt infrastructure — the layer of authenticity between a transaction and a dispute. Success looks like: a school admin generates 10 receipts in a month and a parent can verify one on their phone without creating an account.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, precise, institutional. The brand should feel like a central bank built a product in 2026 — serious and credible, but modern and fast.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / startup-casual: Notion, Loom, Linear (too casual, too startup)
- Warm fintech: Piggybank, Cowrywise color palette (too friendly, not authoritative enough)
- Government e-portal aesthetic: FIRS, NIMC (credible but dated, not premium)
- Glassmorphism / gradient-heavy: anything that trades credibility for visual trickery

## Design Principles

1. **Institutional authority first.** Every surface should feel like it could be printed, stamped, and filed. If it looks like a startup, we've failed.
2. **Verification is the product.** The verification card is the most important screen. It should feel like an official document, not a UI component.
3. **Darkness signals seriousness.** The deep green dark palette communicates that this is infrastructure, not an app. Light weight = light stakes.
4. **Gold is a full stop.** Champagne/gold accents appear at singular moments — the brand mark, key headings, primary actions — never as decoration.
5. **Every word is load-bearing.** No filler copy. No restated headings. Terse, specific, direct.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum throughout. Body text minimum 4.5:1 on all backgrounds. The verification page must be fully readable on cheap Android phones in bright sunlight (high contrast, large touch targets). Reduced motion respected.
